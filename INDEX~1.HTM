Arrival

Turn opposite the District Court, and you'll smell it before you see it.

Hot oil, cardamom, fresh curd. Plastic chairs on the pavement, a counter that never quite stops moving, and a queue that has looked more or less the same for over two decades.

Atmosphere

No hostess. No reservation line. Just a very good queue.

Milan runs the way a good neighbourhood institution should: fast, unfussy, and completely unconcerned with looking impressive. Regulars order without reading the board. Students split a plate of kachori three ways. Lawyers from the court across the road duck in between hearings for a lassi.

It isn't trying to be anything other than exactly what it is — and that's precisely why the city keeps coming back.

Signature Plates

What everyone actually orders

Five things Milan does better than anywhere else on Court Road — the dishes reviewers mention again and again.

Served fresh through the morning rush
Dahi Kachori Most ordered

A crisp lentil kachori broken into cool, spiced curd with tamarind and mint chutney. The dish people drive across town for.

Signature
Onion-spiced, fried to order
Pyaz Kachori

Coarse onion and fennel filling in a flaky shell — the standing-room favourite for anyone in a hurry.

Fast Favourite
Thick, cold, lightly sweetened
Classic Lassi

Churned curd, a little sugar, a lot of body. Ask for it "meetha" or "namkeen" — regulars are split down the middle.

Cool Down
A snack-counter staple
Samosa

Spiced potato, folded tight, fried until the edges shatter. Best with the same tamarind chutney as the kachori.

Everyday Classic
Steamed to order, tempered fresh
Dhokla

Light, tangy, steamed gram-flour squares finished with a mustard-seed tempering — the lighter option on a heavy menu.

Light Bite
Fresh from the bakery counter
Bakery & Sweets

Sandwiches, hot dogs and fresh confections from the "Bakers & Confectioners" side of the counter — the reason it's on every school run.
